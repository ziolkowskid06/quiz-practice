    - id: 1

      question: "COQ"
      answer: "Cost of Quality"
      accept: ["Cost of Quality", "cost of quality"]
      explanation: "All costs incurred over the life of the product by investment in preventing nonconformance to requirements, appraising the product for conformance, and failing to meet requirements (rework)."

    - id: 2
      type: fill-blank
      question: "COPQ"
      answer: "Cost of Poor Quality"
      accept: ["Cost of Poor Quality"]
      explanation: "The financial impact of things going wrong. This includes rework, scrap, warranty claims, and the intangible 'cost' of a damaged reputation."

    - id: 3
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PDCA"
      answer: "Plan-Do-Check-Act"
      accept: ["Plan-Do-Check-Act"]
      explanation: "The Deming/Shewhart cycle for continuous improvement. It is the foundational logic behind almost all quality management systems (ISO, Six Sigma, Lean)."

    - id: 4
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PDSA"
      answer: "Plan-Do-Study-Act"
      accept: ["Plan-Do-Study-Act"]
      explanation: "A variation of PDCA. The 'Study' phase emphasizes checking the results against expectations to gain deeper learning before the next 'Act' phase."

    - id: 5
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SPC"
      answer: "Statistical Process Control"
      accept: ["Statistical Process Control"]
      explanation: "Using statistical methods (like Control Charts) to monitor a process and ensure it operates at its full potential, staying within 'Control Limits'."

    - id: 6
      type: fill-blank
      question: "TQM"
      answer: "Total Quality Management"
      accept: ["Total Quality Management"]
      explanation: "A management approach to long-term success through customer satisfaction. It requires every member of the organization to be committed to continuous process improvement."

    - id: 7
      type: fill-blank
      question: "DMAIC"
      answer: "Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, and Control"
      accept: ["Define Measure Analyze Improve Control"]
      explanation: "The data-driven improvement cycle used in Six Sigma. It is a rigorous process for improving existing business processes by identifying and removing the causes of defects."

    - id: 8
      type: fill-blank
      question: "QFD"
      answer: "Quality Function Deployment"
      accept: ["Quality Function Deployment"]
      explanation: "Often called 'The House of Quality,' this is a method used to translate customer requirements (the 'Voice of the Customer') into specific technical requirements for the product."

    - id: 9
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PDPC"
      answer: "Process Decision Program Chart"
      accept: ["Process Decision Program Chart"]
      explanation: "One of the seven quality management tools. It is used to systematically identify what might go wrong in a plan and develop contingencies to counter those risks."

    - id: 10
      type: fill-blank
      question: "KPI"
      answer: "Key Performance Indicator"
      accept: ["Key Performance Indicator"]
      explanation: "A quantifiable measure used to evaluate the success of an organization or project in meeting objectives. KPIs help a PM keep the team focused on what truly matters for project success."

    - id: 11
      type: fill-blank
      question: "DIRFT"
      answer: "Do It Right The First Time"
      accept: ["Do It Right The First Time"]
      explanation: "A quality philosophy introduced by Philip Crosby. It emphasizes that the cost of quality is really the cost of nonconformance, and prevention is always cheaper than rework."

    - id: 12
      type: fill-blank
      question: "EFTW"
      answer: "Essential First Time Work"
      accept: ["Essential First Time Work"]
      explanation: "The amount of effort and cost required to perform a task correctly on the first attempt. It is the baseline for calculating the Price of Conformance (POC)."

    - id: 13
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PI"
      answer: "Performance Indicator"
      accept: ["Performance Indicator"]
      explanation: "A metric used to monitor the efficiency or effectiveness of specific project activities, often acting as a 'lead indicator' for larger KPIs."

    - id: 14
      type: fill-blank
      question: "POC"
      answer: "Price of Conformance"
      accept: ["Price of Conformance"]
      explanation: "The cost spent to ensure things go right. This includes quality training, thorough planning, and quality audits."

    - id: 15
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PONC"
      answer: "Price of Non-conformance"
      accept: ["Price of Non-conformance"]
      explanation: "The cost of things going wrong. This is the money spent on rework, waste, and fixing defects after they have been created."

    - id: 16
      type: fill-blank
      question: "RI"
      answer: "Result Indicator"
      accept: ["Result Indicator"]
      explanation: "A 'lag indicator' that measures what has actually been accomplished (e.g., number of units shipped) rather than the efficiency of the process itself."

    - id: 17
      type: fill-blank
      question: "QA"
      answer: "Quality Assurance"
      accept: ["Quality Assurance", "quality assurance"]
      explanation: "The process of auditing quality requirements and the results from quality control measurements to ensure appropriate quality standards are being used. QA is process-oriented and proactive — it focuses on preventing defects rather than finding them."

    - id: 18
      type: fill-blank
      question: "QC"
      answer: "Quality Control"
      accept: ["Quality Control", "quality control"]
      explanation: "The process of monitoring and recording results of quality activities to assess performance and recommend changes. QC is product-oriented and reactive — it focuses on finding defects in completed deliverables. QC feeds its results back into QA for process improvement."

    - id: 19
      type: fill-blank
      question: "RTM"
      answer: "Requirements Traceability Matrix"
      accept: ["Requirements Traceability Matrix", "requirements traceability matrix"]
      explanation: "A grid that links product requirements from their origin, through design, development, and testing, to the final deliverable. It ensures that every requirement is met and prevents scope creep by making unauthorized requirements visible."

    - id: 20
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SMART"
      answer: "Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Realistic, Tangible"
      accept: ["Specific Measurable Attainable Realistic Tangible", "specific measurable attainable realistic tangible"]
      explanation: "A mnemonic for writing effective project objectives. SMART criteria remove ambiguity by ensuring each objective is clearly defined, can be measured, is achievable within constraints, is realistic in scope, and is concrete enough to verify completion."
