    - id: 1

      question: "FFP"
      answer: "Firm Fixed Price"
      accept: ["Firm Fixed Price", "firm fixed price"]
      explanation: "A type of fixed-price contract where the buyer pays the seller a set amount, regardless of the seller's costs. It shifts the risk of cost overruns primarily onto the seller."

    - id: 2
      type: fill-blank
      question: "T&M"
      answer: "Time and Material"
      accept: ["Time and Material", "time and materials"]
      explanation: "A hybrid type of contractual arrangement that contains aspects of both cost-reimbursable and fixed-price contracts. They are often used for staff augmentation or when a precise SOW cannot be quickly prescribed."

    - id: 3
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPAF"
      answer: "Cost Plus Award Fee"
      accept: ["Cost Plus Award Fee"]
      explanation: "A contract where the seller is reimbursed for all legitimate costs, plus a fee based on the buyer's subjective satisfaction with the performance."

    - id: 4
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPFF"
      answer: "Cost Plus Fixed Fee"
      accept: ["Cost Plus Fixed Fee"]
      explanation: "The seller is reimbursed for costs and receives a fixed fee calculated as a percentage of initial estimated costs. The fee doesn't change based on actual costs, which discourages the seller from inflating costs."

    - id: 5
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPIF"
      answer: "Cost Plus Incentive Fee"
      accept: ["Cost Plus Incentive Fee"]
      explanation: "Reimburses costs and pays a fee if the seller hits specific performance targets (like finishing early or under budget). Both buyer and seller share the risks/rewards via a 'sharing ratio'."

    - id: 6
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPPC"
      answer: "Cost Plus Percentage of Cost"
      accept: ["Cost Plus Percentage of Cost"]
      explanation: "A contract where the seller's fee increases as their costs increase. This is illegal in many government sectors (like US Federal) because it provides zero incentive for the seller to control costs."

    - id: 7
      type: fill-blank
      question: "FP-EPA"
      answer: "Fixed Price with Economic Price Adjustment"
      accept: ["Fixed Price with Economic Price Adjustment"]
      explanation: "Used for multi-year contracts. It's a fixed-price contract that allows for pre-defined adjustments based on inflation, commodity prices, or currency fluctuations."

    - id: 8
      type: fill-blank
      question: "FPIF"
      answer: "Fixed Price Incentive Fee"
      accept: ["Fixed Price Incentive Fee"]
      explanation: "A fixed-price contract that allows for a higher profit if the seller meets performance targets. It has a 'Ceiling Price,' and any cost above that ceiling is the sole responsibility of the seller."

    - id: 9
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PTA"
      answer: "Point of Total Assumption"
      accept: ["Point of Total Assumption"]
      explanation: "In an FPIF contract, this is the cost level at which the seller has spent so much that they are now responsible for every additional dollar spent. It’s where the contract effectively becomes a Firm Fixed Price contract for the seller."

    - id: 10
      type: fill-blank
      question: "RFI"
      answer: "Request for Information"
      accept: ["Request for Information"]
      explanation: "A document used to gather general information about market capabilities. It’s a research tool used before formal bidding begins."

    - id: 11
      type: fill-blank
      question: "RFP"
      answer: "Request for Proposal"
      accept: ["Request for Proposal"]
      explanation: "Used when the buyer has a problem but doesn't know the exact solution. Sellers propose their unique methodology, technical solution, and price."

    - id: 12
      type: fill-blank
      question: "RFQ"
      answer: "Request for Quotation"
      accept: ["Request for Quotation"]
      explanation: "Used for standard, commodity items (like buying 500 laptops). The focus is almost entirely on price because the technical requirements are already standard."

    - id: 13
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SOW"
      answer: "Statement of Work"
      accept: ["Statement of Work"]
      explanation: "A narrative description of the work to be performed under a contract. It defines the specific tasks, deliverables, and timeline required from the seller."

    - id: 14
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CSOW"
      answer: "Contract Statement of Work"
      accept: ["Contract Statement of Work"]
      explanation: "The final version of the SOW that is officially included as part of a signed contract. It becomes a legally binding document that defines the seller's obligations."

    - id: 15
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPF"
      answer: "Cost Plus Fee"
      accept: ["Cost Plus Fee"]
      explanation: "A broad category of cost-reimbursable contracts where the buyer pays the seller for all actual costs plus an additional fee for profit."

    - id: 16
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPPF"
      answer: "Cost-Plus-Percentage-Fee"
      accept: ["Cost Plus Percentage Fee"]
      explanation: "A specific type of cost-plus contract where the fee is a fixed percentage of actual costs. This is generally avoided as it incentivizes the seller to increase costs."

    - id: 17
      type: fill-blank
      question: "IFB"
      answer: "Invitation for Bid"
      accept: ["Invitation for Bid"]
      explanation: "A request for priced bids for very specific, often standardized requirements. It is commonly used in government 'sealed bidding' where the lowest responsive bid wins."

    - id: 18
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SOO"
      answer: "Statement of Objectives"
      accept: ["Statement of Objectives"]
      explanation: "A high-level document provided to sellers that describes the 'what' but not the 'how.' It allows contractors the flexibility to propose their own Statement of Work (SOW)."

    - id: 19
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SWD"
      answer: "Subdivided Work Description"
      accept: ["Subdivided Work Description"]
      explanation: "A detailed narrative used to authorize and fund work for specific internal cost centers within a larger project."

    - id: 20
      type: fill-blank
      question: "MOCTLS"
      answer: "Market, Organizational, Customer, Technological, Legal, Social"
      accept: ["Market Organizational Customer Technological Legal Social"]
      explanation: "A mnemonic used to remember the various Environmental Factors (EEFs) that must be assessed during project authorization and planning."
