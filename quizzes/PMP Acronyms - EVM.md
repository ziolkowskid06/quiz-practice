questions:
    - id: 1
      type: fill-blank
      question: "AC"
      answer: "Actual Cost"
      accept: ["Actual Cost", "actual cost"]
      explanation: "The realized cost incurred for the work performed on an activity during a specific time period. It is the 'money spent' variable used in Earned Value Analysis to determine cost variance."

    - id: 2
      type: fill-blank
      question: "BAC"
      answer: "Budget at Completion"
      accept: ["Budget at Completion", "budget at completion"]
      explanation: "The sum of all budgets established for the work to be performed. It represents the total planned cost of the project and acts as the benchmark for calculating the Estimate at Completion (EAC)."

    - id: 3
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CPI"
      answer: "Cost Performance Index"
      accept: ["Cost Performance Index", "cost performance index"]
      explanation: "A measure of the cost efficiency of budgeted resources, expressed as the ratio of earned value to actual cost ($EV / AC$). A value of 1.0 or higher is generally considered good."

    - id: 4
      type: fill-blank
      question: "EV"
      answer: "Earned Value"
      accept: ["Earned Value", "earned value"]
      explanation: "The measure of work performed expressed in terms of the budget authorized for that work. It represents the value of the 'physical progress' the team has actually achieved."

    - id: 5
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PV"
      answer: "Planned Value"
      accept: ["Planned Value", "planned value"]
      explanation: "The authorized budget assigned to scheduled work. It represents what the project *should* have cost at a specific point in time according to the schedule baseline."

    - id: 6
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SPI"
      answer: "Schedule Performance Index"
      accept: ["Schedule Performance Index", "schedule performance index"]
      explanation: "A measure of schedule efficiency, expressed as the ratio of earned value to planned value ($EV / PV$). It indicates how closely the project is adhering to the timeline."

    - id: 7
      type: fill-blank
      question: "ACWP"
      answer: "Actual Cost of Work Performed"
      accept: ["Actual Cost of Work Performed"]
      explanation: "An older term largely synonymous with 'Actual Cost' (AC). It represents the actual amount spent to complete work within a specific time period."

    - id: 8
      type: fill-blank
      question: "BCWP"
      answer: "Budgeted Cost of Work Performed"
      accept: ["Budgeted Cost of Work Performed"]
      explanation: "Now commonly called 'Earned Value' (EV). It is the value of the work actually completed, expressed in terms of the approved budget assigned to that work."

    - id: 9
      type: fill-blank
      question: "BCWS"
      answer: "Budgeted Cost of Work Scheduled"
      accept: ["Budgeted Cost of Work Scheduled"]
      explanation: "Now commonly called 'Planned Value' (PV). It is the authorized budget assigned to the work that was scheduled to be completed by a certain date."

    - id: 10
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CV"
      answer: "Cost Variance"
      accept: ["Cost Variance"]
      explanation: "Calculated as $EV - AC$. A positive value means you are under budget, while a negative value means you have spent more than the budgeted value of the work performed."

    - id: 11
      type: fill-blank
      question: "EAC"
      answer: "Estimate at Completion"
      accept: ["Estimate at Completion"]
      explanation: "The projected total cost of the project at the end. It is updated throughout the project based on actual performance. The formula varies depending on whether you expect future performance to be like the past or like the plan."

    - id: 12
      type: fill-blank
      question: "ETC"
      answer: "Estimate to Complete"
      accept: ["Estimate to Complete"]
      explanation: "The expected cost needed to finish all the remaining work. This is a critical metric for a PM to request additional funding or to justify that the remaining budget is sufficient."

    - id: 13
      type: fill-blank
      question: "SV"
      answer: "Schedule Variance"
      accept: ["Schedule Variance"]
      explanation: "Calculated as $EV - PV$. It measures whether the project is ahead or behind the planned schedule in terms of the value of work completed."

    - id: 14
      type: fill-blank
      question: "TCPI"
      answer: "To-Complete Performance Index"
      accept: ["To-Complete Performance Index"]
      explanation: "The cost performance required to finish the remaining work within the remaining budget. If TCPI is greater than 1.0, the team must work more efficiently than they have been to meet the goal."

    - id: 15
      type: fill-blank
      question: "PMB"
      answer: "Performance Measurement Baseline"
      accept: ["Performance Measurement Baseline"]
      explanation: "An integrated scope-schedule-cost plan for the project work. This is the 'yardstick' against which project execution is compared to measure and manage performance."

    - id: 16
      type: fill-blank
      question: "EVA"
      answer: "Earned Value Analysis"
      accept: ["Earned Value Analysis"]
      explanation: "The process of looking at the relationship between Planned Value, Earned Value, and Actual Cost to determine the current status of the project and forecast future results."

    - id: 17
      type: fill-blank
      question: "TAB"
      answer: "Total Allocated Budget"
      accept: ["Total Allocated Budget"]
      explanation: "The sum of all budgets established for the project, which include the Performance Measurement Baseline (PMB) and the Management Reserve (MR)."

    - id: 18
      type: fill-blank
      question: "VAC"
      answer: "Variance at Completion"
      accept: ["Variance at Completion"]
      explanation: "A projection of the budget deficit or surplus at the end of the project. Calculated as $BAC - EAC$. A positive number means you expect to finish under budget."

    - id: 19
      type: fill-blank
      question: "EVM"
      answer: "Earned Value Management"
      accept: ["Earned Value Management", "earned value management"]
      explanation: "A project performance measurement methodology that integrates scope, schedule, and cost baselines to give an objective view of project health. By comparing what was planned (PV), what was accomplished (EV), and what was spent (AC), it enables forecasting of final project outcomes."

    - id: 20
      type: fill-blank
      question: "EVMS"
      answer: "Earned Value Management System"
      accept: ["Earned Value Management System", "earned value management system"]
      explanation: "The integrated management control system that sets the standards for how EVM is applied on a project or program. An EVMS links work scope, schedule, and resource planning to provide reliable, auditable performance data."

    - id: 21
      type: fill-blank
      question: "LOE"
      answer: "Level of Effort"
      accept: ["Level of Effort", "level of effort"]
      explanation: "A type of work activity that does not produce a definitive end product and is measured only by the passage of time (e.g., project management, liaison work). LOE activities always earn exactly what was planned and therefore never show a schedule variance."
