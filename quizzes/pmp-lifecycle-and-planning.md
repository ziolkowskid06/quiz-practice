  - id: 1
    type: "true-false"
    question: "When a project is cancelled due to budget cuts, the Close Project or Phase process can be skipped because no deliverable was produced."
    answer: false
    explanation: "False. The Close Project or Phase process is mandatory for ALL project endings — including cancellation and starvation. Resources must be released, lessons learned captured, and a final project report issued. Skipping formal closure is a governance failure, not an acceptable shortcut."

  - id: 2
    type: "true-false"
    question: "Rolling Wave Planning is the predictive environment's version of Progressive Elaboration."
    answer: true
    explanation: "True. Rolling Wave Planning is the PMI term for progressive elaboration in predictive (waterfall) projects. You plan upcoming work in detail while keeping distant work at a summary level — adding detail as you approach each period. In Agile, the equivalent is Backlog Refinement."

  - id: 3
    type: "true-false"
    question: "In Agile, Backlog Grooming and Backlog Refinement are two different processes with distinct purposes."
    answer: false
    explanation: "False. Backlog Grooming and Backlog Refinement are the same activity — PMI uses both terms interchangeably. Both refer to the process of taking vague User Stories and decomposing them into detailed tasks just in time for the next Sprint. The exam uses both terms as distractors."

  - id: 4
    type: "true-false"
    question: "The Cone of Uncertainty means that estimates are most accurate at the start of a project when all requirements are fresh."
    answer: false
    explanation: "False. The Cone of Uncertainty is widest at project start — uncertainty is highest and estimates may be off by 4x. As decisions are made and information is gathered, the cone narrows and estimates become more precise. This is the underlying principle behind Progressive Elaboration: do not force precision early."

  - id: 5
    type: "true-false"
    question: "PMI treats tailoring as optional — a good practice when time allows, but not required on every project."
    answer: false
    explanation: "False. Tailoring is PMBOK 7 Principle #8: 'Tailor based on context.' PMI treats it as a professional obligation, not a shortcut or optional enhancement. Every PM is expected to deliberately tailor their approach to fit the project. Applying every process from the PMBOK to every project without adjustment is explicitly an antipattern."

  - id: 6
    type: "true-false"
    question: "Fast Tracking is a schedule compression technique that overlaps project phases that would otherwise run sequentially."
    answer: true
    explanation: "True. Fast Tracking = overlapping phases. The schedule shortens but the risk of rework increases — if an earlier phase changes after a later phase has started, the later work may need to be redone. This distinguishes Fast Tracking from Crashing, which adds resources rather than overlapping phases."

  - id: 7
    type: "true-false"
    question: "An Agile (adaptive) life cycle is both iterative AND incremental simultaneously."
    answer: true
    explanation: "True. Agile is unique in combining both properties: iterative (scope is refined through repeated cycles — not defined upfront) AND incremental (value is delivered in functional pieces each Sprint). Predictive is neither. Iterative alone = scope uncertain, refined through repetition. Incremental alone = scope defined, delivered in pieces."

    type: "true-false"
    question: "In an Addition project ending, the project was so successful that it is promoted to a permanent department or line of business."
    answer: true
    explanation: "True. Addition is the 'promotion' ending. The project's success leads the organization to convert the temporary effort into a permanent structure — a new department, program, or value stream. It is the highest form of strategic alignment: the project literally becomes part of the organization's future."

  - id: 9
    type: "true-false"
    question: "Starvation is a valid governance decision where leadership chooses to end a project by gradually reducing resources."
    answer: false
    explanation: "False. Starvation is a governance FAILURE, not a valid decision. It is death by neglect — budget is cut, key people are reassigned, but no formal cancellation decision is made. The PMI Mindset requires a deliberate decision (formal cancellation) followed by Close Project or Phase. Starvation reflects a failure of servant leadership at the sponsor level."

Scope Creep is unauthorized expansion of scope beyond what was approved. The exam tests whether you can distinguish planned refinement (elaboration) from uncontrolled growth (creep)."
