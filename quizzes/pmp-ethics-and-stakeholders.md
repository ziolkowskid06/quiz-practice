  - id: 13
    type: true-false
    question: "The Power/Interest Grid and the Power/Influence Grid use the same two dimensions."
    answer: false
    explanation: "They differ on the second dimension. The Power/Interest Grid plots Power vs. Interest (how much the stakeholder cares about the outcome).   - id: 14

    options: ["Salience Model", "Stakeholder Cube", "Power/Influence Grid", "Power/Interest Grid"]
    answer: "Power/Interest Grid"
    explanation: "The Power/Interest Grid is the baseline communication strategy tool. High Power/High Interest stakeholders are Managed Closely (detailed, frequent updates).     type: multiple-choice

    options: ["Power/Interest Grid", "Power/Influence Grid", "Stakeholder Cube", "Salience Model"]

  - id: 16
    type: multiple-choice
    question: "Two stakeholders are both classified as High Power / High Interest. On a flat grid they look identical. However, one actively champions the project while the other is working to block it. Which model makes this critical difference visible?"
    options: ["Power/Interest Grid", "Power/Influence Grid", "Stakeholder Cube", "Salience Model"]
    answer: "Stakeholder Cube"
    explanation: "The Stakeholder Cube adds a third dimension — Attitude (champion, neutral, or blocker) — to the standard 2D grid. Two stakeholders identical on Power and Interest are managed completely differently if one is a champion and the other a blocker. The Cube gives the PM a tool to visualize and prioritize that distinction."

    options: ["Power/Interest Grid", "Power/Influence Grid", "Stakeholder Cube", "Salience Model"]

This committee has low Power and questionable Legitimacy, but their Urgency is immediate — they are physically stopping the project right now. The Salience Model is dynamic: a stakeholder's Urgency can spike overnight, making them the top priority even with low formal Power."

    answer: false
    explanation: "The Salience Model is explicitly dynamic. This dynamic quality is exactly why it is used for complex or volatile projects."
