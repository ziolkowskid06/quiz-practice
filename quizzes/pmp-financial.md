      question: "BCR"
      answer: "Benefit-Cost Ratio"
      accept: ["Benefit-Cost Ratio"]
      explanation: "A ratio used in project selection. A BCR of 1.5 means for every $1 spent, the project returns $1.50. Anything greater than 1.0 is considered a good investment."

    - id: 2
      type: fill-blank
      question: "LCC"
      answer: "Life-Cycle Costing"
      accept: ["Life-Cycle Costing"]
      explanation: "Looking at the total cost of ownership, from project inception and construction through operation, maintenance, and eventually disposal. A cheaper build that costs more to maintain is often a bad decision."

    - id: 3
      type: fill-blank
      question: "ROI"
      answer: "Return on Investment"
      accept: ["Return on Investment"]
      explanation: "A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment. It measures the amount of return on an investment relative to its cost."

    - id: 4
      type: fill-blank
      question: "ROM"
      answer: "Rough Order of Magnitude"
      accept: ["Rough Order of Magnitude"]
      explanation: "An initial estimate provided very early in the project life cycle (Initiating). It typically has a range of -25% to +75% accuracy."

    - id: 5
      type: fill-blank
      question: "DCF"
      answer: "Discounted Cash Flow"
      accept: ["Discounted Cash Flow"]
      explanation: "A valuation method used to estimate the value of an investment based on its expected future cash flows, which are 'discounted' back to their value in today's dollars."

    - id: 6
      type: fill-blank
      question: "G&A"
      answer: "General and Administrative"
      accept: ["General and Administrative"]
      explanation: "Indirect costs that are necessary for the organization to function but cannot be directly attributed to a single project (e.g., accounting, legal, and executive salaries)."

    - id: 7
      type: fill-blank
      question: "WC"
      answer: "Working Capital"
      accept: ["Working Capital"]
      explanation: "Current Assets minus Current Liabilities. It represents the liquidity of the organization and its ability to fund day-to-day operations during the project."

    - id: 8
      type: fill-blank
      question: "IRR"
      answer: "Internal Rate of Return"
      accept: ["Internal Rate of Return"]
      explanation: "The discount rate that makes the net present value (NPV) of all cash flows from a particular project equal to zero. It's used to compare the profitability of potential investments."

    - id: 9
      type: fill-blank
      question: "P&L"
      answer: "Profit and Loss"
      accept: ["Profit and Loss"]
      explanation: "A financial statement that summarizes the revenues, costs, and expenses incurred during a specific period. PMs in a 'Strong Matrix' or 'Project-oriented' org often have P&L responsibility."

    - id: 10
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CACN"
      answer: "Cost Account Change Notice"
      accept: ["Cost Account Change Notice"]
      explanation: "A formal document used to authorize a budget transfer between different cost accounts, ensuring every dollar is traced and authorized."

    - id: 11
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CER"
      answer: "Cost Estimating Relationship"
      accept: ["Cost Estimating Relationship"]
      explanation: "A mathematical model (like 'cost per square foot') used in parametric estimating to predict costs based on historical data and project variables."

    - id: 12
      type: fill-blank
      question: "DC"
      answer: "Discounted Cashflow"
      accept: ["Discounted Cashflow"]
      explanation: "The calculation of the present value of future cash flows using a specific discount rate to account for the risk and the time value of money."

    - id: 13
      type: fill-blank
      question: "DTUPC"
      answer: "Design to Unit Production Cost"
      accept: ["Design to Unit Production Cost"]
      explanation: "A target-costing approach where the product is designed specifically to meet a pre-determined manufacturing cost, common in high-volume product development."

    - id: 14
      type: fill-blank
      question: "FV"
      answer: "Future Value"
      accept: ["Future Value"]
      explanation: "The value of an investment at a specific point in the future based on a certain interest rate. Formula: $PV(1 + r)^n$."

    - id: 15
      type: fill-blank
      question: "MCCS"
      answer: "Management Cost and Control System"
      accept: ["Management Cost and Control System"]
      explanation: "An integrated framework of procedures used to plan, monitor, and control project costs and resources."

    - id: 16
      type: fill-blank
      question: "ROA"
      answer: "Return on Assets"
      accept: ["Return on Assets"]
      explanation: "A profitability ratio that shows how efficiently a company (or project) is using its assets to generate profit."

    - id: 17
      type: fill-blank
      question: "ROS"
      answer: "Return on Sales"
      accept: ["Return on Sales"]
      explanation: "A measure of operational efficiency that shows how much profit is being produced per dollar of sales."

    - id: 18
      type: fill-blank
      question: "B&P"
      answer: "Bid and Proposal"
      accept: ["Bid and Proposal", "bid and proposal"]
      explanation: "Indirect costs incurred in preparing a bid or proposal for a potential contract. B&P costs are not typically charged to any one project but are instead allocated as an overhead expense across the performing organization."

    - id: 19
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CBR"
      answer: "Cost-Benefit Ratio"
      accept: ["Cost-Benefit Ratio", "cost benefit ratio", "cost-benefit ratio"]
      explanation: "The ratio of a project's total costs to its total benefits. A CBR less than 1.0 is desirable (benefits outweigh costs). It is the inverse of the BCR — where BCR > 1.0 is good, CBR < 1.0 is good. Used in project selection to compare competing investments."

    - id: 20
      type: fill-blank
      question: "CSF"
      answer: "Critical Success Factors"
      accept: ["Critical Success Factors", "critical success factors"]
      explanation: "The limited number of key variables where things must go right for a project or organization to achieve its strategic mission. Identifying CSFs helps a PM focus attention and resources on the areas that truly drive project success."

    - id: 21
      type: fill-blank
      question: "NPV"
      answer: "Net Present Value"
      accept: ["Net Present Value", "net present value"]
      explanation: "The difference between the present value of all cash inflows and all cash outflows over the life of the investment. A positive NPV means the project adds value and should be selected; in project portfolio selection, the project with the highest NPV is generally preferred."
